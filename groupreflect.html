Group Reflection

Liam's Reflection

As a team we have worked more cohesively than in the previous assignment, this is mainly attributed our changes to our minute taking processes and our clear definition of due dates at the start of this assessment period. Overall, we were more consistent on making the meeting times with no person missing more that 2 meetings, this allowed for more live discussion about how we each felt about what direction to take for the next portion of wark that had to be done. There was more varied contribution in the meetings as well, this was helped by splitting up the roles of the chair-person and the minute taker, allowing the chair-person to contribute more. By setting the guidelines that, anyone who misses a meeting must watch the recording and make comment in the group chat, made it so the person not in the meeting could be held accountable for not attending. As we have each be given specific parts to completed within this document, it has helped us stay organised.

Xavier's Reflection

As an overall statement this assignment went more smoothly than the last. This is due to the group's ability to understand what worked and what did not work from the previous assignment. The group talked through our last feedback and concluded that what we needed to work on was communication. We started making sure as a team that our communication was better this time and this led to us having a lot more understanding of each other's situations and deadlines that needed to be met. There were a lot more interferences this time with individuals lives outside the project and this was only a minor issue, and not a fault of any individual. It did cause our meetings to be missing a member often. However, with our communication we were able to mitigate any issues that this could cause as we made sure there was an understanding on what needed to be done and what deadlines needed to be met. The groups willingness to support one another was a surprise and this was a great attribute to the overall cohesion and cooperation of the group. The thing I learned specifically about working in a group is that everyone is willing to support one another provided there is an understanding of the situation, and that communication is clear between the members.

Group Reflection

Communication has significantly improved compared to our assessment. Our primary communication tool is Microsoft Teams’ chat function, in which we are able to get instant messages across all our team members. We feel we are more open and transparent with the progress of our work and able to keep everyone informed should formal meetings be missed by any team members.
Being more active in chats on an almost daily basis allowed us to work more cohesively and ease the workload between the group. Most of our team members have felt less stressed and more focused on tasks needing to be done at specific deadlines we set for the duration of assessments 3 and 5.
Our problem solving and delegation tasks have also improved, which saw the workload evenly distributed to team members based on their strengths. The tool Monday.com also aided in giving a birds-eye overview of the workload that each team member had. Each member could update items on the list and provide insights on any work completed or hurdles faced.
We were also able to work in smaller group settings in these assessments. For example, we saw Sergei and Maxim work together well on the Quickclass website and presentation as we found common work seen in both assessments. Both of these members learnt that they are gifted creatively.
We could improve on spending more time at the planning stage at the beginning of the assessment. We saw some risks in planning week by week during this assessment, which could have resulted in problems not being identified earlier (fail fast, fix quickly), team members not producing work on time or possible work being overlooked.
One thing that was surprising was the way in which our team worked with significant autonomy and agreed on most areas of the assessment. We all had different ideas and were respectful in allowing each team member to communicate their ideas and vote as a group on paths and views we should take up and research.
Our Github repository is updated by all team members in this assessment to allow everyone to practise their HTML skills and work in a different collaborative way outside of our Microsoft Document hosted on Sharepoint.
As a group, we learnt that everyone is independent enough to do their own work without any micromanaging. We are able to come together to collaborate together on this assessment document. We have also found our team members are open to both providing constructive feedback to each other’s work and also assisting each other out on expanding on ideas.
