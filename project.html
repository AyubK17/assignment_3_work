Project Idea

Overview

topic

The main goal of our project is to provide a learning platform called QuickClass, with short courses across a vast range of areas. This learning platform will prompt users to register and create a user profile based on their hobbies, interests and career goals. Students will also be able to input previous courses that they have completed. Based off this information, QuickClass will make recommendations on further courses the student can complete in line with these goals and be assigned a tutor to each course. This tutor will teach and provide support through the learning process and will provide feedback after the final grade on how user went and possible next steps.

This platform will have similar functionality to online study at University, but with short courses that could range as low as 1 to 3 sessions per course. These courses will cover interests and hobbies, career goals, workplace compliance and more. Each subject will have an online content library for the student to access. They may have homework and a quiz at the end to determine if the user has retained this knowledge.

Motivation

The primary motivation behind this platform comes from the desire to learn, which is the consensus of Team Exodus. There are many ways to learn about every subject matter, but often this information is omni-directional, and may not cater for different learning styles. There are many learning platforms that provide educational videos and quiz students on their knowledge, but near to none offer the support from teachers or tutors. It is important for students to be able to ask questions during the learning process and communicate the areas that they may get stuck on. This process will also allow us to modify our training and make sure that is delivered in the best manner possible. This platform offers that opportunity, without the full long-term commitment of a university or TAFE qualification.

Current trends show that online classes are gaining popularity rapidly, and this shows that there is a share in the market for our platform. If we launched this project in the coming months, it would demonstrate to our current and future employers, our ability to work in a team, work to a deadline and demonstrate our creativity in the process.

Description

Aim

The main aim of QuickClass is to provide an online and live learning platform that caters specifically to those that only require short classes to cover specific topics. This is mean filling in a gap in the market for just short online live courses. QuickClass will be able to cater for users who wish to learn new interests and hobbies, such as photography/photoshop. Or to further understand topics they are already familiar with, either professionally or in everyday life. QuickClass will also provide HR and health and safety compliance to any company, in any industry. It will be expected that QuickClass will be available to on computer (both Windows and Mac), all mobile devices and VR to ensure that is fully access able to anyone who has access to any of these devices and an internet connection.

Funding

Qualified Teaching Staff

The teachers employed by QuickClass will be real educators who are professionals in what they teach. They will have a high understanding and be qualified in the courses that they are teaching. This will involve a screening process, similar to a job interview and will guarantee that the teaching staff employed by QuickClass are qualified and provide the best possible education for the students.

Enrolment

Enrolment will be done through the home page of the site. The course that a student wishes to enroll in can be found through a search bar. This will allow for a smooth experience in finding the courses any individual would like to take part in. The overall aim is for a smooth experience in the presentation and this is extended to finding the courses to enroll in. There will also be commonly enrolled in courses on the home page as advertisements to give potential direction to those who are not sure on what they are hoping to enroll in.

Payment

Courses can be purchased in a couple of ways. The first is for personal accounts. A user with a personal account will be asked to make a payment of 20% of the course’s total cost. This is to ensure a guarantee that if a course has been allocated to several people there is already a commitment to the course. If a person does not show up to this course then the money will not be refunded unless that individual reaches out to QuickClass and makes a formal request for a refund. In situations like this there will be compassionate understanding of situations and a refund can be made. Upon completion of the course the rest of the total course cost will be asked to be paid in full, through lump sum or through payment plan. This payment plan will alleviate some of the costs to those who may be financially unable to commit to a full lump sum when the course enrollment is due.
The other payment method is for those with an enterprise account. This is meant for a workplace enrolling members of their staff in a course. These charges will not go to individuals and will instead be sent as a lump sum to the organization who will be required to make a payment in full. This is to ensure a serious commitment from the company.

Types of Accounts

There are two different account types for users of QuickClass. Personal accounts and Enterprise accounts.
Personal accounts are those used by the general public to enroll in courses. These can be linked to multiple services such as Facebook and Google Accounts. The personal account is designed to allow an individual to enroll in the course of their choosing, from anything to a hobby they are interested in, to a skill they are hoping to learn.

Enterprise accounts are set up by companies and registered under their company name. They are able to add multiple users to these accounts under the one company and enroll more than one person in the same course. This allows for a company to easily manage and allocate staff to do certain courses. If a person a staff member is not required or unable to complete a course then they do not need to be signed up and the company can ask for a number of courses making sure that if they want their staff to attend the course enrollment is as smooth an experience as possible.

Types of Courses

QuickClass will offer a range of courses to users. These can include certificates used to further personal development like First Aid and Occupational Health and Safety, to courses designed around hobbies such as Playing Chess or Photography. A wide range of courses will be included with teaching staff enthusiasm and inspiration at the forefront of the learning. The courses designed around personal development will be primarily advertised to companies seeking training for staff, whilst the courses more related to hobbies and personalized skills will be advertised on personal accounts. The courses designed to be more personalized and individual focused will be ranging from multiple levels such as beginner, intermediate, and advanced, meaning if someone was hoping to learn a language and understood the basics, they could enroll in an intermediate course more suited to their needs.

Course Completion

Upon completion of a course that requires a certificate, a certificate of completion will be emailed to that accounts registered emails. For personal accounts, this will be their preferred email, however for enterprise accounts these will be emailed to the registered email of each individual employee that has been provided by the company through their account details. This will allow each staff member to acquire their certificates individually and forward them to whomever they feel appropriate. This way the company can arrange the completion of courses for their staff in a manner they deem suitable.
For courses that do not require a certificate, an email will still be provided with an acknowledgment of completion, to give the user the satisfaction of being told they have done something and can be proud of their achievement.
If the required work did not meet the teachers' standards and requires adjustments then the email will not involve a certificate or acknowledgment of completion but will instead show the user that they need to adjust their work and they are able to re-submit, or email their course teacher for clarification and specific feedback. This will be enforced on all courses but more strictly when certificates of completion are involved as courses requiring certificates will need to be completed with competency and set criteria to avoid legal issues when stating that an individual has an understanding of a specific area. For courses resulting in acknowledgement of completion, the decision to adjust work will be up the discretion of the teacher and whether they feel the participant has a sufficient understanding.

Funding

As this is an educational program, requiring the employment of teaching staff, government funding can be requested to launch the site. This will come under the Victorian State Government Department of Education, as the state government is responsible for tertiary funding for schools, and other educational organizations. Teachers' payment, Management, and IT support costs will come from enrollment, similar to the way a schools payment structure works. NCCD can be contacted for student specific funding that QuickClass would need to organize.

Algorithmic Based Recommendations

QuickClass will aim to incorporate an algorithmic system in order to discern what a person’s study preferences are. This will involve a quiz upon starting a profile asking for details such as what industry the person works in, what hobbies they currently participate in, and what hobbies they have an interest in learning. With this information, courses with similarities will be recommended to the user to guarantee they are gaining the most from their learning on QuickClass. As the users change interest QuickClass will be able make new suggestions based on recent searches and recently participated/watched classes.

We will create a software that will interact with the user and the user will have the impression that he is working with hight level of artificial intelligence.
The hardware we will use are a powerful computer, preferably two screens for comfortable coding. To develop applications for smartphones, we need to use an Android smartphone and another iOS smartphone. An internet connection is also required.

Outcome

Ultimately, QuickClass would be designed, implemented and deployed as a web based ‘app’. Because it is web based, it can be accessed by any device with an Internet connection and will therefore be device agnostic. Users will have the satisfaction of accessing a variety of courses led by qualified teaching staff at a time that is convenient to them. QuickClass gives a bit more of a personalised learning platform, allowing their users the ability to ask questions to their teachers, have courses suggested and attune to their interests and needs based on AI algorithm, get feedback from course work and gain course completion to add to their CVs and resumes if they wish as.
